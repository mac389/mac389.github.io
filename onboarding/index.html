I write this page to describe for those joining my lab the tools and approaches I think one needs to be proficient in to be productive in my lab. My choices reflect my current focuses (computational linguistics and applied mathematics) and training (PhD in computational neuroscience and MD specializing in toxicology). You don’t have to know everything here. Someone focusing on linguistics doesn’t need to know about Hilbert or Banach. Mastery comes from directed learning and focused practice.

Beyond domain-specific knowledge, I also provide resource on getting grants. For better or worse, academic institutions approximate young academics as a function for converting knowledge into money. More established academics may bring other things (patents, patient base, prestige). Few institutions (and departments within those institutions) invest meaningfully in their junior faculty. Having found one that does, I think its important for students to learn about these nontechnical aspects as well.

There are many ways to skin a cat. This document is dynamic and I am open to suggestions.
